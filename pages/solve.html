Food scarcity is a major social issue that harms human health and destabilizes communities. It occurs when regions cannot produce, import, or afford enough nutritious food, leading to hunger, reduced productivity, and social and economic instability. Recent United Nations data shows that over 735 million people experienced chronic hunger in 2023, and about 2.4 billion faced moderate or severe food insecurity, with Sub-Saharan Africa being the most affected region, where more than 20% of the population is undernourished. The causes are complex and include climate-related droughts and floods that reduce crop yields, conflicts that disrupt distribution, economic crises and inflation that raise food prices, and agricultural systems that struggle to meet global demand.
