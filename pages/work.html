ECOPods is based on a closed-loop ecological system designed to produce food, oxygen, and clean water using minimal external resources. The pod integrates several existing technologies into one compact, autonomous unit. It will be implemented in different areas in both extreme and common environments like: space centers, deserts, urban areas.

With that in mind, its size will be thought of according to the amount of food it needs to produce. This is a representation of what it would look like in a small-scale basis.

1. Solar energy collection

  • LED grow lights
  • Pumps and filters
  • Sensors and monitoring systems
  • The algae bioreactor
The system starts with solar panels mounted on top of the pod, supplying electricity to all internal components. This makes the pod self-powered and usable in remote or extreme locations.

2. Water recycling and purification

Water used in plant irrigation is collected, filtered, and purified to be reused continuously. This closed-loop system drastically reduces water waste and allows functioning in dry or isolated environments.
  • Water filters
  • Condensation collectors
  • UV purification
  • Nutrient balancing system

3. Algae Bioreactor

There will be a circular tube running along one side of the pod containing an algae bioreactor. The algae consume CO₂ released by:
  • Plant respiration at night
  • Human breath (if used in habitats)
In return, the algae produce:
  • Oxygen
  • Biomass that can be turned into fertilizer or biofuel
This helps regulate the internal atmosphere of the pod and supports life-support functions for space or isolated environments.

4. Environmental Sensors

Inside the pod, a network of sensors monitors:
  • Temperature
  • Humidity
  • CO₂ and O₂ levels
  • Light intensity
  • pH and nutrient concentration
An onboard controller or simple AI adjusts the environment automatically by:
  • Changing LED intensity
  • Adjusting water flow
  • Activating ventilation or cooling
  • Balancing nutrient levels
This automation keeps the pod stable without human intervention.
